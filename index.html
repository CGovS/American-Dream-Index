Income & Employment

Income is the most direct signal of economic position. The U.S. median household income is $74,580 (Census Bureau, 2022).

📈

Upward Mobility

Harvard economist Raj Chetty's research shows that where you start strongly predicts where you end up — but surpassing your parents is the defining measure of the American Dream. Only about 50% of Americans out-earn their parents (Chetty et al., 2017).

🎓

Education

Education is the strongest predictor of lifetime earnings. Workers with a bachelor's degree earn 84% more over a lifetime than those with only a high school diploma (Georgetown Center on Education and the Workforce).

🏠

Housing & Homeownership

Homeownership is the primary wealth-building vehicle for American families and a cornerstone of the Dream. 65.6% of Americans own their home, though first-time ownership is at a near-50-year low (Census Bureau, 2023).

💳

Financial Security

Financial resilience determines whether setbacks derail your progress. 56% of Americans cannot cover a $1,000 emergency expense without debt (Bankrate, 2024).

🏥

Health Security

Medical debt is a top cause of financial ruin in the U.S. — and the leading driver of bankruptcy. 8.6% of Americans remain uninsured and 1 in 4 skip needed care due to cost (CDC, 2023).
